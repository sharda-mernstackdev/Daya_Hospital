Hemorrhoids

At Daya Hospital, we provide comprehensive and advanced treatment options for hemorrhoids, ensuring patient comfort and effective relief. Our team of experienced specialists is dedicated to diagnosing and treating hemorrhoids with the latest medical techniques.

Our Treatment:

Non-Surgical Procedures:

  • Rubber Band Ligation: A minimally invasive procedure where small rubber bands are placed around the base of internal hemorrhoids to cut off blood supply, causing them to shrink and fall off.
  • Sclerotherapy: Injection of a chemical solution into hemorrhoidal tissue to shrink hemorrhoids.
  • Coagulation Therapy: Use of laser or infrared light to harden and shrink hemorrhoids.

Surgical Procedures:

  • Hemorrhoidectomy: Surgical removal of severe or recurrent hemorrhoids, performed by our skilled surgeons with a focus on patient comfort and quick recovery.
  • Hemorrhoidopexy (Stapling): A procedure that involves stapling to remove hemorrhoids and reposition the remaining tissue to prevent prolapse.

General FAQs

Hemorrhoids are swollen veins in the lower rectum and anus, which can cause discomfort, pain, and bleeding. They can be internal (inside the rectum) or external (under the skin around the anus).
Hemorrhoids can be caused by straining during bowel movements, chronic constipation or diarrhea, sitting for long periods, pregnancy, obesity, and a low-fiber diet.
Common symptoms include pain and discomfort, itching and irritation, swelling around the anus, bleeding during bowel movements, and a lump near the anus which may be sensitive or painful.
Hemorrhoids are diagnosed through a physical examination and may include a digital rectal exam. Additional procedures like an anoscopy or sigmoidoscopy may be used to diagnose internal hemorrhoids.
Treatment options range from conservative methods like dietary changes and sitz baths to medical treatments such as rubber band ligation, sclerotherapy, and coagulation therapy. Severe cases may require surgical procedures like hemorrhoidectomy or hemorrhoidopexy.
